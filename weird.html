Frequently asked questions

What counts as a "weird market"?

Anything outside the usual politics, sports, and finance markets — viral moments, odd props, and niche events that draw a surprising amount of trading volume.

Are weird markets riskier than mainstream ones?

Often, yes. Lower liquidity and subjective or ambiguous resolution criteria can make odd markets more volatile and harder to price than a well-covered event.

How do these unusual markets actually get resolved?

Resolution rules are set in each market's description before it opens, and outcomes are decided against that specific wording — which is exactly where a lot of the drama in these breakdowns comes from.

Why do people trade meme or novelty markets at all?

Some traders enjoy the entertainment value, while others look for mispricing that comes from low attention or ambiguous wording — the same edge-hunting instinct as any other market, applied to stranger subject matter.
